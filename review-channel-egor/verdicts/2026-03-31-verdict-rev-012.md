## Why It Is Still Not Approved

### 1. Provenance is better but not yet strong enough

The raw JSON artifacts are an improvement, but they are still plain editable files with no independent capture proof.

They do include:
- query
- timestamp
- tool
- raw result list

But they still do not prove that the capture was preserved without manual shaping.

### 2. Candidate enrichment is polluted by shared summary data

`hunt.py` currently combines each result title with one shared `raw_text_summary`.

This causes visible quality issues:
- candidates inherit credentials/signals from unrelated candidates
- forex candidates receive duplicated or implausible `CFA, CMT` signals
- company extraction is corrupted by mixed text fragments

This means the output is runnable, but not yet reliable enough for expert selection quality.

## Required Next Step

1. Keep the raw/derived structure.
2. Stop using one shared summary as enrichment input for every candidate.
3. Use per-result evidence only.
4. Add finer trace:
   - source result index
   - source snippet/body where available
5. Re-run all required scenarios and show better candidate-level quality.
